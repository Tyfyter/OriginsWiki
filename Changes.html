Changes to Vanilla Content

Origins makes some changes to pre-existing content in order to add more options, encourage a natural progression of added Origins content and to improve sensibility with the lore. These changes may also touch up on the rather neglected areas of Terraria for further diversity. It is additionally worth noting that more changes may be implemented or removed in the future as issues are addressed and when necessary for further development.

If you are looking for recipe changes to vanilla items click here.

Equipment

Advanced weapon functionality in PVP - Weapons and accessories with homing and other proximity effects (like that of Magic Tripwire) now work properly in PVP.

Explosive damage type - In order to make the Demolitionist class, all of the weapons that were considered explosives in vanilla Terraria have been reclassified under the Explosive damage type. Few vanilla weapons still classify as Ranged while also classifying as an explosive.

Harpoon - With interest of a new ranged sub-class idea, we wanted to change the fundamental characteristics of the Harpoon weapon. So, we instead turned the Harpoon weapon into a consumable item that acted as ammunition for harpoon guns. Here is the new Harpoon entry.

Mining armor - Because Mining armor lacked any offensive capabilities and made the most sense as an explosive armor set, it gained additional boosts to benefit demolitionists and thus acts like a pre-boss armor for this class. The Helmet provides a 3% explosive critical strike chance increase, the Shirt increases explosive damage by 5% and the set bonus decreases explosive self-damage by 20%.

Proximity Mine Launcher - With the introduction of the Resizable Mines, we thought it would make more sense if the Proximity Mine Launcher used them as ammo alongside rockets. Rockets can still be used as we didn't want to remove any previous functionality of the Proximity Mine Launcher.

Rain armor - With Rain armor having no effect in vanilla and the added dangers of Riven rain and other potential effects from rain, Rain armor grants immunity to the Rain. Alongside this set bonus, it dons many new generic bonuses, which make it particularly useful in the Rain.

Royal Gel - To match items like Strange Tooth and Protozoa Food, Royal Gel now summons a slime minion to fight for the player, which counts as summon damage and thus benefits from any summon-related bonuses.

Storm Spear and Thunder Zapper - With the addition of the Static Shock debuff in Origins, it was decided that the Storm Spear and Thunder Zapper could likely benefit from inflicting this debuff alongside the Felnum arsenal.

Volatile Gelatin - In order to make Volatile Gelatin more useful and match the bonuses we've given our own accessories like Speed Booster, this item's attack now benefits from damage bonuses provided by the Wrath Potion or any other accessories. Furthermore, it is now considered for explosive damage and allows the player's hits to coat enemies in slime that eventually explodes.

Ebonwood, Shadewood, and Pearlwood armors - Ebonwood, Shadewood, and Pearlwood armor have received boosts to their defense values. Ebonwood and Shadewood both having 2 in their helmets, and 3 in their breastplates and greaves (8 total). Pearlwood has 6 in the helmet and greaves, and 7 in the breastplate (19 total).

Tiles

Trees - All trees (except for gem trees) now drop Bark and Tree Sap, which both can be used to craft Rubber. Tree Sap can additionally be used to craft some food items.

Entities

Bats - All bats now drop the Bat Hide material, which can be crafted into Leather.

Demolitionist - The Demolitionist now sells Explosive Powder at anytime rather than solely in Hardmode. It does not interfere with the obtainability of Exploding Bullets and the change allows for more explosive weapons to be crafted.

Dryad - The Dryad has a button in her dialogue box 'Purify' that can cleanse the player of their Assimilation for an expensive price.

Skeleton Merchant - The Skeleton Merchant now sells Black Ink at anytime in order to make obtaining the Journal easier.

Eater of Worlds - With the addition of the Demolitionist weapons, it was necessary to reduce the damage reduction given to the Eater of Worlds in Expert Mode to explosives. The Eater of Worlds' damage reduction is reduced to 50% in Expert Mode and remains at 80% in Master Mode.

Boss Immunities - With stun mechanics provided by vanilla's Heart Arrows, and Origins' Rasterized and Toxic Shock debuffs, we felt it was proper to give every boss a strong resistance against this mechanic entirely for balancing reasons. In most cases, it is entirely possible to stun-lock certain bosses and artificially remove all difficulty provided with avoiding their attacks. With the right set-up, however, it is still possible to stun-lock some enemies in the game.
